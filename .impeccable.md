## Design Context

### Users

Primary users are maintainers, platform teams, documentation owners, and coding agents working inside real repositories. They arrive with one urgent job: determine where a change belongs, what evidence to read, which files may be edited, and which checks prove the work. The journey must also remain understandable to non-technical evaluators discovering the AgentsKit ecosystem.

### Brand Personality

Precise, trustworthy, and quietly impressive. Doc Bridge is an operational bridge under the AgentsKit parent brand: technical without being cryptic, confident without hype, and polished without decorative noise. The emotional outcome is relief—ownership is explicit, the next command is obvious, and documentation becomes executable evidence.

### Aesthetic Direction

Editorial infrastructure: a refined documentation system that treats handoffs, routes, and proof as the visual material. Use the AgentsKit logo family and ecosystem relationships, ink-tinted neutrals, one controlled green/blue operational accent, crisp diagrams, purposeful motion, and asymmetric explanatory layouts. Support light and dark environments with WCAG AA contrast and reduced-motion behavior. Avoid neon AI gradients, generic glowing dark dashboards, repetitive card grids, stock illustrations, and decoration that does not teach the product.

### Design Principles

1. Show the handoff before explaining it: real `startHere`, `editRoots`, `checks`, and `humanDoc` evidence lead the story.
2. Progressive disclosure: a useful 60-second path first, complete contracts and recipes one step deeper.
3. One source, many consumers: humans, LLM routes, CLI, MCP, CI, and chat must visibly converge on the same index.
4. Operational honesty: local/backend provenance, maturity, optional peers, errors, and missing documentation are explicit.
5. Accessible by construction: semantic hierarchy, keyboard-first interactions, readable measure, resilient mobile layouts, meaningful visuals, and reduced motion.
